# 1. Product Vision

Ivy's Challenge is a personal English learning product built for calm, sustained vocabulary growth rather than fast, shallow repetition. It is intended to feel like a private learning space where vocabulary, reading, review, and reflection connect into one coherent experience.

Its learning philosophy is based on deliberate exposure, repeated recognition, active recall, and gradual transfer from isolated words into meaningful reading. The product should help the learner move from "I have seen this word" to "I can understand it, remember it, and use it with confidence."

It is different from traditional vocabulary apps because it does not treat learning as a noisy task loop driven by streak pressure, cartoon rewards, or fragmented drills. Instead, it emphasizes a refined study environment, structured repetition, thoughtful review, and a stronger relationship between word learning and real reading.

# 2. Design Philosophy

Ivy's Challenge should feel:

- Clean
- Minimal
- Premium
- Personal English learning space

The visual and interaction standard should reference Apple-level simplicity and high-end editorial design. The interface should feel intentional, quiet, spacious, and elegant. Every learning action should appear focused and meaningful rather than busy, gamified, or decorative.

# 3. Core Modules

## Vocabulary Library

The Vocabulary Library is the main index of the learning system. It organizes the learner's word base into chapters and gives a clear overview of progress, learning status, and entry points into study modes.

## Word Card Mode

Word Card Mode is the focused study mode for building recognition and memory of individual words. It should support concentrated one-word-at-a-time review with minimal distraction.

## Word List Mode

Word List Mode is the structured table view for scanning, spelling, and reviewing multiple words within the same chapter or collection. It should support comparison, repetition, and visible progress across a group of words.

## AI Reading

AI Reading turns learned vocabulary into short reading experiences. It should connect isolated vocabulary learning to contextual comprehension and encourage learners to encounter words in meaningful usage.

## Personal Vocabulary Bank

The Personal Vocabulary Bank stores words that the learner personally wants to revisit, especially unknown or important words discovered during reading and review. It should function as a curated, personal layer on top of the main vocabulary system.

## Collocation System

The Collocation System captures how words naturally appear with related words and phrases. Its purpose is to move the learner beyond dictionary-level meaning toward more natural language sense and usage.

## AI Review Coach

AI Review Coach provides interpretation of learning performance. It should help the learner understand weak areas, review patterns, and progress trends, so review becomes guided rather than random.

# 4. Vocabulary Learning System

The vocabulary system should be organized by chapters so the learner can progress through a structured body of words instead of a flat word list. Each chapter should feel like a manageable study unit with visible progress.

Every word should have trackable learning status over time. The system should reflect whether a word is newly seen, partially remembered, unstable, or relatively mastered, so the learner can understand what still needs attention.

The seven-box memory system is a core review structure. It should represent repeated encounters over time and make progress visible at the word level. The purpose is not only to count exposure, but to encourage stable long-term retention.

Recognition logic should distinguish between:

- recognized with confidence
- uncertain / partially remembered
- unknown

These distinctions matter because they reflect different memory states and should influence how a word progresses or regresses in review.

The system should include a reset mechanism for words that have become unstable after repeated failure. A learner should be able to see that certain words need to restart rather than artificially remain marked as mastered.

Mastered words should not disappear permanently. The product should support periodic review of previously mastered vocabulary so retention remains active and false mastery is reduced.

# 5. Word Card Requirements

Each word card should include:

- word
- phonetic
- pronunciation
- part of speech
- Chinese meaning
- example sentence

The word card should support a hide/show mechanism so meaning is not always revealed immediately. This preserves the learner's chance to recognize the word before seeing the answer.

Recognition buttons should allow the learner to mark whether the word is recognized, uncertain, or unknown. These actions should feel fast, lightweight, and central to the study flow.

# 6. Word List Requirements

The word list should present a chapter or word collection in a clear structured table.

Each row should include:

- number
- word
- phonetic
- meaning
- spelling practice
- seven status boxes

The word list should support both scanning and interaction. It should allow the learner to quickly review many words, test spelling, and update memory status without leaving the list context.

# 7. AI Reading Requirements

AI Reading should generate short articles based on words the learner has already studied. The reading should feel natural and coherent rather than like a random vocabulary dump.

Generated articles should intentionally use learned vocabulary as the foundation of the text. They should also include a small number of unknown words so reading remains slightly challenging and supports growth.

The unknown-word ratio should stay around 5-10%. This keeps reading within a productive difficulty zone: understandable enough to continue, but challenging enough to expand vocabulary.

Unknown words discovered during reading should be collectable through direct interaction. Double clicking an unknown word should add it into the Personal Vocabulary Bank for later review.

# 8. Future Roadmap

## Vocabulary Garden

Vocabulary Garden should visualize vocabulary growth in a more emotional and motivating way. Its role is to make long-term accumulation feel visible without reducing the product to a toy.

## AI Personal Teacher

AI Personal Teacher should become a more active and personalized learning guide. It should move beyond reporting and begin offering tailored review direction, encouragement, and study suggestions.

## Learning Analytics

Learning analytics should become more sophisticated over time, helping the learner understand progress quality, weak categories, review frequency, and long-term retention patterns.

## Gamification

Gamification may be introduced carefully, but it should remain restrained and aligned with the premium identity of the product. Any game-like layer should support motivation without making the product feel childish, noisy, or generic.
